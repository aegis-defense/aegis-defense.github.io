Ballistic Missile Defense

About

AEGIS is a sophisticated missile-defense simulation platform featuring a fully interactive 3D front end built in Unity, providing real-time visualization of missile trajectories, intercepts, and global engagements. Behind the scenes, a custom physics engine written in C# powers realistic simulations, while C#, .NET, and SQLite form the core of the tech stack and database system. Users can build scenarios, place launch sites and defenses, and analyze outcomes through layered visual tools and detailed metrics. This unified workflow supports research, training, and advanced concept development with high technical fidelity.

Features

High-Fidelity Missile & Defense Physics Engine

AEGIS is built around a unified, physics-based simulation core that models realistic missile trajectories, intercepts, and multi-threat engagements. Users can create or modify missiles from a built-in database, test hypothetical “what if?” weapons, and evaluate defensive systems under configurable conditions. This level of realism enables researchers, DoD analysts, and contractors to experiment with next-generation concepts before they ever reach the field.

Interactive 3D Globe with Intelligence-Grade Overlays

Scenarios unfold on an interactive 3D globe where users can place launch sites, defenses, and sensor networks, then visualize outcomes in real time. Layered views—such as blast radius, population density, core infrastructure, satellite grids, and weather effects—turn raw simulation data into intuitive visual insight. This lets operators quickly understand strategic impact on civilians, critical assets, and the broader battlespace.

End-to-End Scenario Design, Training, & Collaboration

AEGIS is a complete workflow tool, not just a visualizer. Users can build scenarios from scratch in the Scenario Editor, run simulations with time acceleration and interception tools, then review detailed analytics, logs, and metrics. Save/load and export features support replay, briefing, and sharing across teams—making AEGIS ideal for training, academic research, and proof-of-concept pitches for new defense technologies.
